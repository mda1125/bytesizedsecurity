Title: Why You Should Request Your LexisNexis Consumer Disclosure Report and Freeze Your Credit Today

TL;DR: LexisNexis maintains a consumer file on you beyond your credit report. Request it for free, review it for errors or signs of identity theft, and freeze your credit at Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. If you have children, freeze their credit too. All of it is free and takes about 15 minutes total.

Most people know about credit reports from Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. But there's another data file out there with your personal information, and few people know it exists.

LexisNexis, one of the largest data analytics companies in the world, maintains a Consumer Disclosure Report on nearly every adult in the United States. This report is separate from your credit report and contains a different set of information: real estate transactions, property ownership records, liens, judgments, bankruptcy filings, professional license information, and historical addresses linked to your name.

Insurance companies, employers, landlords, and financial institutions use LexisNexis data to make decisions about you. If the information in your file is inaccurate, or if someone has been using your identity, you face consequences without ever understanding the reason.

## How to Request Your Free Report

Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), you have the right to request a copy of your Consumer Disclosure Report from LexisNexis at no cost. You have three options:

1. Online: Submit a request through the official Consumer Portal at https://consumer.risk.lexisnexis.com/consumer. After identity verification, you'll receive a letter via U.S. Mail with instructions to access your report online.

2. By Mail: Download and complete the Printable Request Form from the portal and mail it to:
LexisNexis Risk Solutions Consumer Center
P.O. Box 105108
Atlanta, GA 30348-5108

3. By Phone: Call 1-866-897-8126 to request your report.

## What to Look For

When you receive your report, review it carefully for:

- Addresses you've never lived at
- Property transactions you didn't make
- Liens or judgments you don't recognize
- Professional licenses not belonging to you
- Any other records seeming unfamiliar

If you find errors, dispute them directly with LexisNexis. Inaccurate information in their system is likely affecting your insurance rates, rental applications, employment background checks, or financial decisions being made about you.

If you see information clearly belonging to someone else using your personal details, your identity has been compromised.

## Freeze Your Credit at All Three Bureaus

After reviewing your LexisNexis report, the next step is freezing your credit at all three major credit bureaus:

1. Equifax: https://www.equifax.com/personal/credit-report-services/credit-freeze/
2. Experian: https://www.experian.com/freeze/center.html
3. TransUnion: https://www.transunion.com/credit-freeze

How a credit freeze works: When you apply for a loan, credit card, or any new line of credit, the lender pulls your credit report. If your credit file is frozen, the lender's request is blocked. No credit check means no approval, which means the fraudulent application is denied on the spot.

This stops the most common form of identity theft: new account fraud. A thief with your Social Security number, name, and date of birth opens credit cards, takes out loans, and runs up charges in your name. A credit freeze makes all of this extremely difficult.

Key facts about credit freezes:

- It's completely free (mandated by federal law since 2018)
- You do it online at each bureau's website
- Each freeze takes about five minutes to set up
- You receive a PIN or password to temporarily lift the freeze when you need to apply for credit yourself
- It does not affect your credit score
- It does not prevent you from using your existing credit cards or accounts

## Don't Forget Your Children

If you have children, freeze their credit too. This is one of the most overlooked steps in personal security.

Children are attractive targets for identity thieves because they have clean Social Security numbers and nobody is monitoring their credit. A thief uses a child's information to open accounts, and the fraud often goes undetected for years. Most victims don't find out until they turn 18 and apply for their first credit card, student loan, or apartment.

Each credit bureau has a process for freezing a minor's credit. You'll need to provide documentation proving you're the parent or guardian, but the process is straightforward and free.

## The Bottom Line

Requesting your LexisNexis Consumer Disclosure Report and freezing your credit at all three bureaus takes about 15 to 20 minutes total. This small investment of time gives you visibility into what data companies have on you and a strong layer of protection against identity theft.

Don't wait until something goes wrong. Check your report. Freeze your credit. Protect your kids. It's free, it's easy, and it's one of the most effective things you do to protect your identity.
